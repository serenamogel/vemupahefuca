    Who is Michael Stone?

    Michael Robert Stone is the first of two sons birthed to Rob and Jackie Stone. He was born on the 7th day of June 1990 in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. His younger brother Mark Stone, who is also an ice hockey player is the captain of the Vegas Golden Knights of the National Hockey League (NHL).

    Stone played two seasons with the Calgary Hitmen of the WHL during his junior ice jockey career. He was later selected by the Phoenix Coyotes in the third round, 69th overall in the 2008 NHL Entry Draft. He played two more seasons with the Hitmen before signing an entry-level contract with the Coyotes.

    On February 18, 2012, Stone made his NHL debut for the Coyotes against Dallas Stars. He recorded 11:31 of ice-time in a 2-1 overtime win against the Stars. He notched his first goal on March 10, 2012 in a 3-o win over the San Jose Sharks. Over the 2012-13, Stone managed just a goal and two assists in 13 games.

    Due to the 2012-13 lockout, Stone spent the early part of the season with the Portland Pirates, the Coyotes’ AHL affiliate and rejoined the Coyotes after the lockout ended. After his return, he scored 5 goals and provided a further 4 assists for 9 points in 40 games.

    After two straight seasons of success, where he achieved post a career-high 36 points for the Coyotes, Stone was signed to a one-year, US$4 million contract extension with the Coyotes, avoiding salary arbitration on July 29, 2016.

    Stone was traded to the Calgary Flames in exchange for a 2017 third-round pick and a 2018 conditional fifth-round pick on February 20, 2017. He recorded six points in 19 games to close out the season.

    During his time with the Flames, Stone began well until he was plagued with injuries. He sat out the entirety of the Flames’ postseason games. The Flames decided to buy out his final year contract, leaving him an unrestricted free agent.

    He rejoined the Flames for two more seasons before deciding to retired from professional ice hockey on July 5, 2023.
