    James Krause’s Fight Schedule

    At the age of 19, James Krause found MMA. He had an 18-1 record as an amateur fighter. During that period, he won two championships and was recognised as the country’s top amateur fighter at one point.

    He made his professional debut in 2007 with Kansas’ Titan Fighting Championships. Kevin Hengler, his first opponent, was defeated by technical submission in the first round. For the following several years, James Krause fought all across the Midwest, compiling an undefeated 10-0 record with all of his matches ending in stoppages.

    James Krause joined with the World Extreme Cagefighting organisation in the spring of 2009. His professional debut would be against the famous Donald Cerrone. Cerrone’s original opponent, Krause, was a late substitute. Cerrone would win the opening round by rear naked choke.

    James Krauses’ next battle would be against Ricardo Lamas in November 2009 at WEC 44. Lamas defeated Krause, and Krause was eventually expelled from the company after losing two fights.

    Krause joined the Bellator Fighting Championships the following April (2010). Toby Imada was his first opponent in the organization’s Lightweight Tournament. Krause would lose via armbar and would be eliminated from the lightweight competition.

    He is expected to fight at Bellator 16 next. Due to an injury, James Krause would be forced to withdraw from that card.

    James resigned from the Titan Fighting Championship after a brief run with the Bellator organisation. He would defeat Nathan Schut via TKO after landing a stunning head kick in his debut fight.

    At Titan Fighting Championship 19, he will face veteran fighter Clay French. Krause would be defeated by French in a split decision.

    Over the next year, James Krause would win three more fights in various local fighting groups. His best chance with the UFC was right around the corner.

    In March of 2012, James Krause was chosen as one of the fighters for The Ultimate Fighter Live event. A victory in this event was essential to advance to the next season of The Ultimate Fighter. In the bout, James Krause was knocked out in the first round by Justin Lawrence.

    On June 30, 2012, James Krause returned to the Resurrection Fighting Alliance and defeated another Ultimate Fighter audition veteran, Amir Khillah, via split decision. His next bout in the RFA concluded in 31 seconds by knockout, with James Krause prevailing once more. Following his submission of his next opponent, Joe Jordan, at RFA 5, James Krause would call out UFC matchmaker Joe Silva and request that he “give him a call.”

    At UFC 161, James Krause would make his UFC debut against veteran Sam Stout. After a back-and-forth fight, James Krause would eventually finish Stout with a guillotine in the third round. This victory would also earn him the bonuses for Submission of the Night and Fight of the Night.

    Krause’s next UFC battle, against Bobby Green, would conclude on a contentious note. Green repeatedly kicked Krause below the belt, resulting in a one-point deduction by referee John McCarthy. Green would then strike another kick to the belt line of James Krause, crumpling him. Green would win the TKO after John McCarthy declared it a lawful blow.

    At UFC 173, James Krause will battle Jamie Varner. Varner suffered a broken ankle in the first round and was unable to continue, so the bout ended in a TKO for James Krause.

    At UFC 178, James Krause will meet Jorge Masvidal. Masvidal would be declared the winner by a unanimous vote.

    James Krause would lose another decision, this time a split decision, to Valmir Lazaro at UFC 184.

    James Krause would turn things around at UFC on Fox 16 in July 2015, when he successfully defeated Daron Cruickshank by submission in the first round.

    James would win his next fight at UFC Fight Night 83 against Shane Campbell. He’d be given a unanimous decision.

    The Ultimate Fighter 23 Finale was supposed to feature James Krause vs. Ross Pearson, but he was withdrew from the bout for unclear reasons. The fight against Pearson was rescheduled for November 2016 at UFC Fight Night 99, but James Krause was forced to withdraw due to a hamstring rupture.

    In 2017, it was revealed that James Krause would compete in The Ultimate Fighter Redemption’s 25th season. He would be the first pick for Team Dillashaw. On the show, he would go 2-1.

    At the start of 2018, it was revealed that James Krause would be joining Squad Head Coach Stipe Miocic’s coaching team for the next season of The Ultimate Fighter.

    After appearing as a participant and later as a coach on The Ultimate Fighter series, James Krause would return to the UFC in July of 2017 to face Tom Gallicchio in the TUF 25 Finale. Krause would prevail by unanimous decision.

    James Krause would triumph over Alex White in his next fight at UFC Fight Night: Stephens vs. Choi. He’d win a unanimous decision.

    In August of 2018, he defeated Warlley Alves via TKO at UFC Fight Night 135. At UFC on ESPN+ 22, he faced Sergio Moraes. He would defeat Moraes in the third round and earn the Performance of the Night prize.

He was defeated by a split decision. The fight received the Fight of the Night bonus. Later, it was determined that Krause and his team would appeal the split judgement owing to a suspected conflict of interest because one of the judges who helped sway the decision in Giles’ favour had a link to Giles’ training squad.
