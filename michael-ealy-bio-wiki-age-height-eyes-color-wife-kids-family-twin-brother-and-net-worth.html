    Michael Ealy Barbershop

    Ealy came to prominence after appearing in the 2002 comedy film ”Barbershop” where he played as reformed street thug Ricky Nash alongside former N.W.A’s crew member Ice Cube, the Black-ish star  Anderson Anderson, Keith David. He also reprised his character on its sequel in 2004 Barbershop 2: Back in Business.

    Before that, Michael was featured alongside Anthony Hopkins and Chris Rock in the 2002 action comedy film Bad Company. Later he appeared in the second installment of the Fast and the Furious film series, 2 Fast 2 Furious in 2003 playing the role of Slap Jack.

    The following year, he appeared opposite rapper DMX in Never Die Alone. In the same year, Michael appeared in one Maria Carey’s hit song video  “Get Your Number”.

    Michael Ealy and Halle Berry

    Likewise, he portrayed  Tea Cake while the play protagonist Halle Berry acted as Janie Crawford in the 2005 film Their Eyes Were Watching God based on Zora Neale Hurston’s 1937 novel. The film is produced by the show host Oprah Winfrey and Quincy Jones.

    Michael Ealy Sleeper Cell

    On December 14, 2006, Ealy was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for his role in Sleeper Cell: American Terror in the category Best Performance by an Actor in a Mini-Series or Motion Picture Made for Television.

    In December 2008 he was featured in the movie Seven Pounds alongside Will Smith as Ben Thomas. He also starred as the male lead in Beyoncé’s “Halo” music video, and as CIA Field Officer Marshall Vogel in the ABC television series FlashForward.

    Michael Ealy The People Speak

    Ealy also appears in the limited-edition coffee table book (About Face) by celebrity photographer John Russo, published by Pixie Press Worldwide. He is currently working on independent writing projects.

    In 2009, Ealy performed in The People Speak, a documentary feature film that uses dramatic and musical performances of the letters, diaries, and speeches of everyday Americans, based on historian Howard Zinn’s A People’s History of the United States.

    Michael Ealy Takers

    In 2010, Ealy appeared as attorney Derrick Bond in the second season of The Good Wife. He co-starred in the 2010 action movie Takers as Jake Attica and appeared as Travis Marks in the USA Network’s original series Common Law about two cops who have to go to counseling because they argue too much. Common-Law premiered following Fairly Legal on Friday, May 11, 2012.

    Michael Ealy Think Like a Man

    Ealy portrayed  Dominic in the 2012 ensemble comedy Think Like a Man and its 2014 sequel, Think Like a Man Too, and appeared as “Papa Joe” in the 2012 inspirational film Unconditional. He appeared opposite Think Like a Man co-star Kevin Hart in the 2014 remake of About Last Night.

    Michael Ealy Almost Human

    In 2013, Ealy signed on to play co-lead “Dorian” in the Fox television series Almost Human. The sci-fi police procedural takes place in the year 2048 and follows the relationship between two cops as they struggle to solve futuristic crimes that involve complex technology.

    Ealy depicts the android “Dorian”, an older, “DRN” android model that is considered to be less reliable due to its artificial emotions and that is tasked with protecting his partner John Kennex (Karl Urban). The show aired for one season on Fox from 2013 to 2014.

      Michael Ealy The Following

    In 2015, Ealy played serial killer “Theo” in season 3 of the Fox television series The Following. He starred as Eric Warner in the second season of the ABC mystery crime series Secrets and Lies, which aired in the fall of 2016. Since 2017 Ealy has starred in the fourth season of Being Mary Jane opposite Gabrielle Union.

    Michael Ealy Sickness

    Michael is stepping up to fight cancer, and ETonline has an exclusive first look at his new print PSA for November’s Pancreatic Cancer Awareness Month.

    “I lost someone close to me to this horrible disease,” Ealy says. “I experienced firsthand how devastating pancreatic cancer can be for those who battle it, as well as for their loved ones.

    What scares me is that anyone can get pancreatic cancer, yet there is no cure and there is no early detection test. People need to know that research is our best hope to beat this deadly disease and save lives.”
